Background

Jason Berke, Esq. is the principal attorney of the Law Offices of Jason Berke PC. He has been handling landlord/tenant and real estate litigation matters for over 10 years, with a practice built around careful statutory analysis, motion practice, and trial-ready preparation.

He attended Pace School of Law and Appalachian School of Law on scholarship, graduating Magna Cum Laude.

Representative Accomplishments

  • Fraudulent conveyance: Summary judgment granted on claim alleging actual intent to defraud involving transfer of real estate from husband and wife as tenants by the entirety to wife only. On reargument, the court awarded $500,000+ money judgment and counsel fees rather than reverting the transfer where the sole debtor was the husband.
  • Federal default judgment vacated: Motion granted to vacate a 14-year-old SDNY default judgment exceeding $180,000 (plus statutory interest) after a second remand from the Second Circuit. Matter resolved for $25,000 following an FRCP 68 offer—approximately $375,000 less than the accrued judgment with interest.
  • Divorce settlement enforcement: Summary judgment granted on breach of a divorce settlement for failure to remove client/husband from a mortgage. In related post-judgment litigation, the court agreed contract interpretation required time-of-the-essence performance. Escrow funds of $62,500 were returned to the client.
  • Foreclosure defense (issue of first impression): Motion to dismiss a diversity foreclosure action granted where plaintiff failed to serve RPAPL 1304 notice on the borrower. The court held RPAPL 1304 was required even if the borrower no longer resided in or owned the property. Mortgage was ultimately forgiven after further motion practice.

Prior results do not guarantee a similar outcome. Outcomes depend on the facts, law, and procedural posture of each case.

Approach

Landlord/tenant and real estate cases often hinge on details: what was served, when it was served, what the lease requires, what the rent ledger shows, and how the timeline matches statutory requirements. The firm focuses on building a clear narrative and supporting it with documents and procedural rigor.

  • Early issue-spotting: service, jurisdiction, and notice defects
  • Document-first strategy: leases, riders, notices, ledger, communications
  • Motion practice: targeted motions to narrow or resolve the case
  • Trial readiness: organized evidence and witness preparation
