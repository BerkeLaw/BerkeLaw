What a Yellowstone Injunction Is

A Yellowstone injunction is a court order that can toll a cure period and maintain the status quo, allowing a commercial tenant to litigate the merits of an alleged default without risking forfeiture of the lease. These applications are often time-sensitive and require careful papers: affidavits, the lease, notices, and proof of the tenant’s willingness and ability to cure if necessary.

Why Timing Matters

Courts often scrutinize whether the application was brought promptly and before termination. Missing the window can eliminate the remedy. If you receive a notice to cure or notice of termination, speak with counsel quickly and preserve all documents.

  • Review the lease’s notice and cure provisions
  • Evaluate the alleged default and curability
  • Prepare emergency motion papers where warranted

Typical Requirements Courts Consider (General)

Exact standards depend on facts and precedent; the firm evaluates your posture and urgency.

  • Existence of a commercial lease
  • Receipt of a notice to cure/termination and threat of forfeiture
  • Application made before termination
  • Tenant’s ability and willingness to cure (where curable)
